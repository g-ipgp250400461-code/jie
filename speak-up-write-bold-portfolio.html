A Critical Review of the 'Speak Up! Write Bold!' English Language Competition

IPG Kampus Batu Lintang, Kuching  ·  Prepared by Harvard Ngu Wei Jie  ·  26 July 2026

1.0 Introduction

The English Language Competition entitled 'Speak Up! Write Bold!' was successfully held on 15 July 2026 at Auditorium Taza, IPG Kampus Batu Lintang, Kuching. Organised by the English Language Society (ELS) under the patronage of the Campus Director, the competition was designed to cultivate students' English language proficiency and confidence through two intellectually stimulating events: a Public Speaking Contest and a Creative Writing Challenge. This competition holds considerable significance for the campus community, as it represents a proactive initiative to extend English language learning beyond the conventional classroom setting. This report provides a critical evaluation of the competition, assessing its overall effectiveness, highlighting key strengths, identifying areas that require improvement, and proposing actionable recommendations for future events.

2.0 Summary of Events

The competition commenced at 8.00 a.m. and was structured into two main sessions. The morning session featured the Public Speaking Contest, in which 30 selected participants each delivered a five-minute persuasive speech on pre-assigned topics pertaining to current global and national issues. Contestants demonstrated admirable levels of preparation, with many delivering eloquent and well-reasoned arguments that captivated the audience. Spectators responded with enthusiastic applause, creating an energetic and encouraging atmosphere throughout the session.

The afternoon session was dedicated to the Creative Writing Challenge, during which participants were given 90 minutes to craft either an original short story or poem inspired by a theme that was revealed only at the commencement of the session. The element of surprise added an exciting dimension to the challenge, testing participants' ability to think critically and creatively under pressure. The event culminated in a Grand Prize-Giving Ceremony at approximately 5.30 p.m., celebrating the outstanding achievements of all participants.

3.0 Critical Evaluation

3.1 Strengths

The competition demonstrated several commendable strengths that contributed to its overall success. Firstly, the event attracted an impressive turnout of over 150 participants and audience members, reflecting the strong interest and enthusiasm among students towards English language activities. This level of participation surpassed initial projections and is indicative of a growing appreciation for the English language within the campus community.

Secondly, the Public Speaking Contest served as a compelling showcase of students' oral communication abilities. Several contestants delivered speeches of exceptional quality, exhibiting not only linguistic proficiency but also remarkable composure, persuasive rhetoric, and critical thinking skills. This demonstrated that students at IPG Kampus Ilmu Khas possess significant potential in English oral communication.

Thirdly, the decision by the judging panel to provide personalised, constructive feedback to each contestant following their performances was greatly valued. This practice transformed the competition into a genuine learning experience, allowing participants to identify their strengths and areas for growth, thereby aligning the event with its core educational objectives.

3.2 Areas for Improvement

Despite its successes, the competition was not without shortcomings. The most notable issue was the significant time overrun of approximately 90 minutes, which resulted in the prize-giving ceremony commencing well beyond the scheduled time. This inconvenienced a number of audience members and highlighted the need for more rigorous time management protocols.

Additionally, the event was hampered by recurring technical difficulties with the public address system during the morning session. These interruptions disrupted the momentum of several speeches and, at times, affected the audience's ability to clearly hear contestants, which is a critical concern for a public speaking competition.

Furthermore, participation from external campuses fell short of expectations, with only two other IPG campuses contributing representatives. Given the competition's aspiration to foster inter-campus engagement and camaraderie, this limited external participation represents a missed opportunity to broaden the event's reach and impact.

4.0 Recommendations

In order to elevate the quality and impact of future competitions, the following recommendations are proposed. First and foremost, a dedicated time-keeper should be appointed to monitor and strictly enforce the schedule throughout the event. Clear time signals, such as visual countdown timers or designated warning cues, should be implemented to ensure all sessions conclude within their allocated time slots.

Secondly, a comprehensive technical rehearsal should be conducted at least one full day prior to the competition. This should include thorough checks of all audio-visual equipment, with contingency plans prepared in the event of unexpected technical failures on the day of the competition.

Thirdly, the outreach process for external campus participation should commence significantly earlier. Formal invitations, accompanied by detailed information packages, should be disseminated to prospective campuses no later than two months before the event. This would afford campus coordinators sufficient time to organise internal selection processes and adequately prepare their representatives.

5.0 Conclusion

In conclusion, the 'Speak Up! Write Bold!' English Language Competition was a commendable and largely successful inaugural inter-campus event that meaningfully contributed to the English language learning culture at IPG Kampus Batu Lintang. Despite minor logistical and technical challenges, the competition fulfilled its primary objectives of enhancing students' communicative competence, fostering creativity, and inspiring a love for the English language. It is earnestly hoped that this competition will be institutionalised as an annual event at the campus, serving as a vibrant and enduring platform for students to celebrate and advance their English language abilities. With the implementation of the proposed recommendations, future editions of 'Speak Up! Write Bold!' have every potential to become a flagship English language event not only within IPG Kampus Batu Lintang, but across the broader IPG network.
